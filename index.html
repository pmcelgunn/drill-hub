Setup · 1 ball + 1 hand towel per pair

QBs pair up 7 yards apart.

How it runs

(a) 5 towel-drill reps — full throwing motion snapping a towel, freeze the finish. (b) 5 one-knee throws — back knee down, front knee 90°, perfect spirals. (c) 5 quick-feet catch-and-release throws — reset feet, return the ball fast with a clean spiral.

Coaching cues

  • Hips fire first, then shoulders, then arm — the arm is last
  • Level head: 1 inch of head movement = 2 inches off at release
  • Elbow ~90° at the top; finish with the WRIST FLICK — ball rolls off index toward the pinky side
  • Pinky side of the hand turns down on the follow-through
  • Self-diagnose: wobble = timing, sail = early head, dirt ball = late hips

10U version

Towel + one-knee only; coach checks elbow height standing beside.

12U / age-up version

Add 5 med-ball rotational scoop tosses (Tuesdays only, 48+ hrs before games); film one slo-mo rep per week and compare release compactness month over month.

Punch Plant Quick-Release
7 min · Glazier Clinics / Tom House

Setup · 2 cones, 1 WR partner

QB in shotgun stance, non-throwing foot forward, back-foot toe just behind front heel. WR at 5-6 yds on a hitch/quick out.

How it runs

On 'hike,' QB takes ONE punch step back with the lead foot, plants, throws. Target: ball out under 1.0 second. On 'not open' call, QB pushes back for depth with short cutting-grass steps and hits target #2.

Coaching cues

  • Toes, knees, hips all point at the target on the plant
  • Back ankle roughly perpendicular to the target line
  • Feet never stop moving; eyes stay level through the punch
  • Quicker feet = quicker release — fix the feet, not the arm

10U version

Start from a static pre-set base: just plant-and-throw to a stationary coach; add the punch step once the release is clean.

12U / age-up version

Coach calls '1' or '2' mid-drop so the target is unknown; stopwatch every rep; fastest-accurate-release leaderboard. This is the 5-second-clock weapon.

Three-Step Fire (Rhythm = Route Timing)
8 min · TeamGenius / NFL FLAG / X&O Labs
▶

Setup · 2 drop cones, 1-2 WRs

Two cones mark exact drop landing spots. WR alternates slant / 5-yd out / curl.

How it runs

Shotgun 3-step rhythm drop (big step, two quick). Ball out ON the plant of step 3 for slant/out; plant + one hitch for curl. Ball arrives as the WR's break finishes.

Coaching cues

  • Each drop step hits the same spot every rep — film from behind
  • Last drop step and the WR's break are synchronized
  • Throw to the spot the break creates — if you wait to SEE open, you're late
  • Weight loads the back hip on the plant, drives through the front knee

10U version

Throw to an orange landmark cone at the catch point on rhythm — no WR needed.

12U / age-up version

Mix route calls so the drop rhythm must match the concept; add a trail defender so the ball must beat the flag-puller to the spot.

Triangle Progression Read (1-2-3 + Checkdown)
8 min · X&O Labs / Quincy Avery / Whitfield
▶

Setup · 3 cones or 3 receivers

Three options form a strong-side triangle: deep at 12, intermediate at 8, checkdown at 3. Coach stands downfield as the defender.

How it runs

QB takes his 3-step drop; coach silently covers one or two options by stepping toward one. QB works 1 → 2 → 3 with a short hitch between each and throws the first open one. Full cycle inside 4 seconds.

Coaching cues

  • Eyes lead the feet — feet re-point at each read
  • NO receiver-lock: locking on = interceptions
  • Hitch-and-a-half between reads is your internal clock
  • The checkdown is a WIN — a 3-yard completion keeps the drive alive at the midfield line

10U version

Two reads only (1 → checkdown); coach covers option 1 obviously.

12U / age-up version

Coach disguises late (moves after the snap like a sitting zone defender); add an 'alert' deep shot when the top is vacated; other QBs count the 5-second clock out loud.

Rail Shot / One-Motion Quick Draw
6 min · Elite 11 Finals

Setup · Net/bucket targets, extra ball

QB ready with ball at carriage. Coach with a hand-shield anywhere on a 90° arc, 8-15 yds away.

How it runs

Coach flashes hands; QB resets feet to the new target line and releases IN ONE MOTION — no re-cock, no false step. 8-10 rapid-fire reps.

Coaching cues

  • Hips carry the ball to the target — never drag the arm across the body
  • Compact release: shorten the loop
  • Shoulders close then open through the throw; finish with the wrist flick
  • Bad spiral = speed the sequence UP, don't slow it down

10U version

Targets limited to a 45° arc in front; underhand cone-flash cue.

12U / age-up version

Add a 'cover' call — coach flashes then yanks the target and QB must NOT throw (interception prevention). Track make % and release time season-long.

Four-Stage Pocket Slide vs 5-Yard Rusher
8 min · Emory Wilhite / George Whitfield / Kurt Benkert
▶

Setup · 4 cones (5x5 pocket), pool noodle optional

4 cones mark a 5x5 pocket. Rusher starts exactly 5 yards away (league blitz depth). WR runs a drag or sits at 8 yds.

How it runs

Four stages across the season: (1) rusher from a KNOWN side — predetermined slide and throw; (2) two possible rushers, known movement; (3) one rusher, UNKNOWN path — read and react; (4) fully live — slide, eyes downfield, throw inside 4.5s.

Coaching cues

  • Two-inch slides — tight and efficient, never panic-bail
  • Eyes NEVER drop to the rusher
  • Two hands on the ball — he's swiping at it and your flags
  • Knees stay bent in the slide; toes stay pointed downfield so you can throw NOW

10U version

Stages 1-2 only; rusher at 3/4 speed; noodle chases but can't touch.

12U / age-up version

Stage 4 + the free-run read: when the rusher over-runs the arc, QB legally tucks through the vacated lane (free run vs blitz — costs none of his 2 runs). Score: throw = 1, smart free-run = 2, flag pulled = -1.

Hot-Hands Blitz Beater
7 min · USA Football / flag adaptation
▶

Setup · 1 WR (hot slant), 1 RB (swing), 1 defender

WR on a 3-yd hot slant, RB swinging to the flat. Defender at 7 yds; on some snaps he creeps to the 5-yd blitz mark pre-snap, on others he stays.

How it runs

QB scans pre-snap. Creep = 'HOT!' call → snap → ball out to the slant before the rusher covers 5 yards (~1.5s). No creep = normal 3-step rhythm throw. Randomize.

Coaching cues

  • The pre-snap plan IS the play — know your hot answer before 'hike'
  • Catchable ball slightly in front, chest-high, so the WR runs away from the puller
  • Never take a deep drop vs a blitz — depth is death with no blockers
  • Run this from the 5-yard line often: it IS your 1-point conversion play

10U version

Coach announces 'blitz' or 'no blitz' out loud pre-snap; QB executes the matching answer.

12U / age-up version

Rusher disguises (creeps then backs out); add the RB as a second hot option and pick the side AWAY from the blitzer.

Safety-Count Call-Out Game (1-High vs 2-High)
3-4 min opener · USA Football coverage formula / Football MBA
▶

Setup · 3-4 players as a defense

QB faces away while coach arranges 3-4 defenders into a shell. No throwing needed — perfect while arms warm up.

How it runs

QB turns and has 3 seconds to call: 'ONE HIGH — middle closed — best throw is [out/comeback/corner]' or 'TWO HIGH — middle open — best throw is [seam/middle].' Then coach sends a WR in motion: defender travels = 'MAN', stays = 'ZONE', and QB names the beater.

Coaching cues

  • Same litany every time: 1) count safeties, 2) short-side corner, 3) blitz threat
  • Motion is the lie detector: chase = man, stay = zone
  • Say it OUT LOUD — verbalizing is the test
  • Confirm post-snap by the 3rd drop step off the deep defender

10U version

Two pictures only (1-high vs 2-high), 5 seconds to answer.

12U / age-up version

Live: QB drops and throws the named beater vs the shown shell while the defense disguises (2-high rotating to 1-high at snap). This is the 14U-level skill.

Sprint-Out / Throw-on-the-Run Ladder
7 min · NFL FLAG / Will Hewlett
▶

Setup · 3 arc cones, WR or bucket target

Arc of 3 cones bending outside either hash. WR runs a matching flood route (out at 6 or comeback). Run BOTH directions.

How it runs

QB catches the snap, sprints the arc gaining width with shoulders squaring downhill, throws off the run at cone 3 — never planting flat-footed. 4 reps right, 4 left.

Coaching cues

  • Toes and knees lead to the target — hips shift, shoulders square BEFORE release
  • Downhill at release; never drifting backward
  • Nose of the ball slightly up on the out-breaking throw
  • Weak side: extra half-beat to square shoulders — don't sling across your body

10U version

Jog-tempo arc, stationary target, strong side only until clean.

12U / age-up version

Trail rusher pursues the arc; decide inside 5 seconds — throw the out or (blitz rep) tuck upfield for the free run. Rep from the actual no-run-zone spots: sprint-out passing IS this league's short-yardage offense.

Layer Ladder (Touch vs Drive)
7 min · TeamGenius / Elite 11 grading
▶

Setup · Net/chair at 6, coach at 10, bucket at 18

Three targets stacked on one line: drive window at 6 yds (waist-high), coach with raised hand at 10 (the lurker), bucket/hoop at 18 (the drop-in).

How it runs

Three-throw sets: (1) DRIVE the 6-yd window flat and hard; (2) LAYER over the raised hand, dipping in front of the deep bucket; (3) DROP the 18-yarder in with full touch. Rotate QBs every set.

Coaching cues

  • Same footwork every throw — the change is release point and finish, not a new motion
  • Touch throws release earlier and higher; drive throws finish long
  • Level eyes on all three
  • Score it 3-2-1 by difficulty — season leaderboard

10U version

Two layers only (drive at 5, touch at 12); bigger targets (trash can, hula hoop).

12U / age-up version

Random order calls with one drop each; then live — WR runs the stacked route vs a real underneath defender. The layered throw over a sitting zone defender is THE throw that beats older kids.

🙌 Offense — WR · 13 drills

Station template: 2 min hands warm-up → 6-8 min movement drill (routes/releases) → 4-5 min compete drill (contested/traffic). Every rep ends with a catch and a tuck.
Hand-Position Rapid Fire (9-Spot)
2 min warmup · Competitive Drive / STACK / Emporia St.
▶

Setup · 1 ball per pair

Pairs, 5-7 yds apart.

How it runs

Partner throws to 9 zones: high left/middle/right, mid left/right, low left/middle/right, plus over-the-shoulder. 3 catches per zone, swap.

Coaching cues

  • Diamond (thumbs together) above the chest; pinkies together below the waist
  • Catch with fingertips AWAY from the body — never body-trap
  • See the point of the ball through your hands
  • Every catch finishes with a violent tuck: catch it, pluck it, TUCK it

10U version

Underhand tosses; call the zone before the throw; 1-count freeze staring at the ball in the hands.

12U / age-up version

No calls — read ball flight; add one-hand catches; partner throws on a late 'BALL!' cue.

Snap Drill (Late Eyes)
5 min · Jake Brown (Glazier) / Emporia St.
▶

Setup · 2-3 balls

Receivers 10 yds from thrower, eyes AWAY from him.

How it runs

Ball is already in the air when coach yells 'BALL!' — receiver snaps head around, locates, frames, catches.

Coaching cues

  • Exaggerate eye speed — the head SNAPS
  • Frame the ball: hands ready before the eyes find it
  • Secure and tuck violently

10U version

Stationary receiver, soft toss, longer flight.

12U / age-up version

Running away from the thrower, over-the-shoulder: extend hands, elbows together, find the ball in the last split second — this is your fade/go-ball timing builder.

Break–Drive–Line Speed Cut Lane
8 min · Drew Lieberman (Sideline Hustle) / Gunter Brewer
▶

Setup · 2 cones, QB/coach

Cone at LOS, cone at 8-10 yds (break point). Two lines so kids break both directions.

How it runs

Sprint the stem selling vertical (low pads, eat up space), then run the out/dig with the 3-part cadence: BREAK (violent direction change) – DRIVE (round the corner 1.5-2 yds) – LINE (plant and snap flat to the QB). Ball arrives as the head comes around.

Coaching cues

  • Full-stride steps through the break — don't chop
  • Arms KEEP PUMPING — no airplane arms
  • THE EYES ARE THE LAST THING OUT OF THE BREAK
  • Never drift upfield after the line step
  • Chest over toes — the torso pulls you through the break

10U version

5-6 yd breaks; walk the 3-part pattern first; cone on the Drive arc as a physical target.

12U / age-up version

Chad Johnson step audit — max 3 steps in the break, coach counts; add a trailing flag-puller at the break; convert to the 4-step Speed Drop at 10-12 yds.

Bam-Step Slant & Snap-Down Curl
7 min · Riley-Kolste / Lieberman
▶

Setup · 2 cones, QB

LOS cone and break cone at 3-5 yds (slant) or 8-10 (curl). QB throws on rhythm.

How it runs

Slant reps = Bam Step: stick the outside foot, jam the inside elbow back, break flat at 45°. Curl reps = 2-Step: brake step + sink hips, plant, drive back downhill to the QB.

Coaching cues

  • Sink the hips ON the brake step, not after
  • Snap the head WITH the break
  • Break as flat as needed to beat the defender to the ball
  • Attack the football with hands away from the body — never wait on a curl

10U version

Cones only, no defender; coach holds a hand up as the throw window.

12U / age-up version

Add a squat defender with leverage — break AWAY from leverage using a Pressure Step stem (stem at the shoulder you'll break away from); then live vs a DB.

Cushion-Killer Stem & Stack
8 min · Kelsey Pope (AFCA) / vIQtory
▶

Setup · 25-yd lane, 1 DB

DB 7 yds off (half-speed shadow at first).

How it runs

Attack the DB's midline at FULL speed (kills cushion, keeps two-way go), stem into the leverage shoulder, win an edge, then STACK — cut onto the DB's toes so he's in trail — and sprint through a landmark before looking for the ball.

Coaching cues

  • Eat up space — no slow-play off the line
  • Get to half-a-man; never run around the DB wide
  • After the win, work back ON TOP of him — the stack makes the QB's throw easy
  • Drive phase first; eyes come late

10U version

Stem/stack tag game: receiver wins by stacking within 10 yds, DB wins by staying square.

12U / age-up version

Live deep ball with over-shoulder pinkies-together catch; route conversion rule: DB stays square and shallow → snap it off into a comeback (the 5-second-clock bail-out).

Setup · 1 ball, matched pairs

Two receivers 3 yds apart facing coach 10-12 yds away. Pair kids of similar height.

How it runs

On 'Hit,' both release; coach throws a jump ball; both attack and high-point it. Winner boxes out with the Gator Roll spin-out after the catch.

Coaching cues

  • Jump TO the ball — meet it at its highest point, never wait
  • Two hands, diamond over the face
  • Shield with the off arm; never give up your chest
  • Land, spin out, tuck — finish every rep
  • Hand-fight early, ball-fight late: hands stay active into the catch point

10U version

One receiver + one non-jumping shadow; underhand lob to a spot.

12U / age-up version

Back-shoulder and fade balls in a 7-yd sideline box: outside release, stay inside the ball, catch over the outside shoulder. Add the RIP round — loser tries to rip the ball out for a 2-count after the catch.

Setup · 1 defender, QB

Receiver at LOS; defender at 5 yds picks a side (leans inside or outside) as the receiver stems; QB at flag rhythm depth.

How it runs

5-yd stem at the DB's midline, then read: DB inside → pivot/whip out; DB outside or soft → sit or snap in. Pivot = plant, drop hips, reverse flat along the LOS.

Coaching cues

  • One decision, made early, at 100% — never double-guess
  • Pivot low, chest over toes
  • Show your hands to the QB immediately out of the break
  • QB and WR read the SAME leverage cue — that's the timing glue

Coach calls 'in!' or 'out!' at the stem instead of a body-lean read.

Silent read vs live DB; add the Jerk route (slant-out-slant) as the counter once DBs jump the pivot.

Setup · 3-4 zone defenders (cones/parents), QB

3-4 defenders in a 20x20 box, each owning a zone. Receiver runs a dig/curl/drag through them.

How it runs

Cross the box, throttle down in the open window between defenders, snap around, show numbers and hands, catch, burst upfield.

Coaching cues

  • Run in grass, SIT in grass — sprint through occupied zones, settle in empty ones
  • Open your hips to the QB, not the sideline
  • Slide with a scrambling QB — deep routes work back to him

10U version

Zones marked with hula hoops or cone squares — literally sit in the box.

12U / age-up version

Defenders shift zones on cadence so the window moves; 5-second clock — receiver learns to uncover late for a QB in trouble.

Catch-Tuck-Juke Gauntlet (Flag YAC)
7 min · Emporia St. / Jake Brown, flag-adapted
▶

Setup · Cone box 10x10, 2 flag-pullers

Receiver catches a hitch at 5 yds, then enters a 10x10 juke box with 2 staggered pullers.

How it runs

Catch → tuck → SNAP the head upfield → beat puller #1 with a juke/dead-leg, puller #2 with burst or spin, score through the end cones.

Coaching cues

  • Catch it, tuck it, put it away, SNAP YOUR HEAD — the head-snap is the drill
  • Make the move 2 yds BEFORE the defender — space is your armor
  • Accelerate OUT of every move
  • Hips away from the puller; arms high, never drifting down to the flags

10U version

One puller; pre-called move ('juke left!').

12U / age-up version

Two live pullers + a chase defender from behind; run as a scored competition.

▶

Setup · Sideline lane 2 yds wide

Cones mark a sideline lane 2 yds wide, 10 yds long. Receiver runs an out or corner toward it; QB throws to the boundary.

How it runs

Catch at the boundary keeping feet in bounds. Teach two-feet 'shotgun feet' even in a one-foot league — it ages up.

Coaching cues

  • Catch FIRST with hands away from the body; feet second
  • Shotgun feet: both toes tap fast
  • Eyes on the ball through the catch — FEEL the line, never look down
  • Drag the toe on hard-breaking outs

10U version

Stationary on the line, coach lobs; kid calls 'tap-tap!' as feet land.

12U / age-up version

Full-speed 10-yd speed out with a trailing DB — the sideline is the second defender.

Distraction / Traffic Catch + Rip-Away
7 min · Emporia St. / Jake Brown Contested Shuffle
▶

Setup · 2 crossing lines or hand-shield

Two lines cross in front of the catch window, or one teammate stands a yard in front waving hands (no contact — perfect for flag).

How it runs

Receiver runs a drag/in through the crossing traffic; ball is thrown through the noise; catch, tuck, burst. Then the RIP round: defender shadows shoulder-to-shoulder, receiver frames and attacks the ball, then RIPS it away violently to simulate the strip battle.

Coaching cues

  • Zoom your eyes to the point of the ball THROUGH the bodies
  • Hands late but ready; squeeze the fingers at the catch
  • Rip THROUGH the ball, elbow over the top — win it twice
  • Expect to make the GREAT catch, not the routine one

10U version

Distractor holds a still hand-shield beside the window; slower ball.

12U / age-up version

Two crossing receivers, deepest gets the ball (mirrors your mesh concepts); add a defender high-pointing to knock it down — trains both sides.

3-Step-Rhythm Route Tree Circuit
8-10 min · Chad Johnson / NFL FLAG — QB-station crossover
▶

Setup · QB rotation, cones

QB takes a 3-step rhythm drop; receivers rotate through a called mini-tree: hitch, slant, out, option, corner/post, go-with-stack.

How it runs

Every route thrown ON TIME — ball out between 2.5-3.0s. Coach counts '1-2-3-BALL' out loud. One rep every ~30 seconds.

Coaching cues

  • Route depth is a CONTRACT with the QB — no freelancing
  • Break steps ≤3 — count them
  • Eyes last out of the break; hands up immediately
  • Covered? Still finish — uncover back to the ball by 4.5s

10U version

Tree = hitch, slant, out only; coach stands at the throw spot as a metronome.

12U / age-up version

Full 15-route flag tree with double moves (chair, stop-n-go, slant-go) — allowed only after the kid wins with the single move. Defender plays 7 yds off and jumps the first break.

Setup · 2 cones 15 yds apart

2 cones east-west 15 yds apart; players start on the right cone; coach as QB.

How it runs

On 'Hike!' run across, catch, tuck. Drop the ball at the far cone, cut back, catch #2 coming back. At the start cone, drop it, cut upfield, catch a deep lobbed ball in stride.

Breadbasket Mesh & Fake Series ⭐
8 min · NFL FLAG / MOJO — upgrades Patrick's handoff series
▶

Setup · 2 LOS cones, QB pairs

QB + RB pairs (add a center if available). RB aligned at real game depth/angle behind QB.

How it runs

Rotate 3 reps each: 2-step handoff, 3-step/stretch handoff, 1-step fake, stretch fake. RB hits the mesh AT SPEED every rep and sprints 10 yds after — or carries out the fake 10 yds hard.

Coaching cues

  • Breadbasket: arm closest to the QB is ALWAYS on top
  • QB places, RB clamps — no snatching, no swinging
  • Never slow into the mesh — take it without decelerating
  • THE FAKE IS A REAL CARRY WITHOUT A BALL — sell it, lean forward, sprint

10U version

Walk → jog → run tempo ladder; freeze-check the breadbasket before adding speed.

12U / age-up version

Live edge rusher at the mesh; counter action (fake one way, hand the other); eyes UP through the mesh — call out the coach's finger count downfield.

Setup · 1 ball each, 10-yd lanes

10-yd lane per player.

How it runs

(a) Stationary arm-switch reps; (b) hop forward switching carry arm each hop; (c) 10-yd carries switching the ball to the sideline arm at a mid-cone; (d) trailer round: a chaser attacks the ball AND swipes at flags from behind while the carrier keeps arms high and legal.

One-Cut Lane (One Move, Then North)
7 min · Football Advantage / Footwork King step-count
▶

Setup · 5 cones

Start cone → stop cone at 5 yds → two exit cones 1 yd left/right → finish cone 5 yds beyond.

How it runs

Sprint, ONE jump-cut/plant-cut off the stop cone to either exit, immediately re-accelerate through the finish. Alternate sides, then go reactive: coach points late — cut opposite his point.

Coaching cues

  • Plant OUTSIDE your frame, shoulders level, hips drop
  • Cut angle as close to 90° as you can — decelerate off the outside edge of the feet
  • COUNT THE STEPS in the redirect — fewest steps wins
  • Celebrate the burst OUT of the cut, not the cut itself

10U version

Pre-called direction, no ball, then add the ball.

12U / age-up version

Live shadow defender in a 2-yd lane behind the cone; add a second cut 5 yds later — two defenders = two single moves, never two moves on one defender.

Coach D's Move-Menu Box ⭐
8 min · Flag Football with Coach D (Patrick owns the pack)
▶

Setup · 4 cones (10x10), 1 defender

10x10 box, defender in the middle; runners enter one end, must exit the far side.

How it runs

Rounds by move family — Get Skinny / Dip / Burst / Spin / Cut — then a free-choice round. Defender live for flags.

Coaching cues

  • WHEN-TO-USE RULES: defender square → burst or cut; reaching/lunging → dip or get skinny; boxed in → spin; near boundary → get skinny
  • ONE move, then go — dancing lets the swarm arrive
  • Dip changes flag altitude, spin changes flag direction, skinny shrinks the target — all LEGAL protection
  • Spin is the legal stiff-arm: as they grab, the flag rotates away

10U version

Defender at walking speed; moves pre-called.

12U / age-up version

Two staggered defenders (one move each); add a no-run-zone stripe where only skinny/burst are allowed — no dancing in the NRZ.
